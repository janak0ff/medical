Hiv and Aids
Urology & Infectious Disease
Time
41mins : 41sec

Full Explanation

HIV/AIDS - HIV is a sexually transmitted infection (STI), found in circulating blood lymphocytes, plasma, semen, body fluids and in all the tissues of the body. These viruses destroy the T4 helper cells that are vital for immune responses to pathogens, bringing about a vulnerability to diseases in the patient.

Myocardial Infarction

Cardiovascular Medicine
Time 11min : 36sec

Myocardial infarction (MI) is the irreversible damage to heart muscles secondary to prolonged ischemia. This usually results from an imbalance of oxygen supply and demand.
This animation discusses the anatomy of heart, myocardial infarction, its main cause - atherosclerosis, symptoms, first aid, and diagnosis including coronary angiography and blood tests. This presentation also deals briefly on treatment, action of drugs, complications and self-care guidelines related to MI.

Endocrinology
Time 12min : 54sec

Type II Diabetes :- A disease marked by high levels of glucose in the blood, Type 2 diabetes occurs when either the body does not produce enough insulin or when the cells does not respond to insulin.
